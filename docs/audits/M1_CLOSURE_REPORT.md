# M1 Closure Report

## 1. Milestone Status

M1 CLOSED WITH NON-BLOCKING NOTES.

M1 is semantically complete at Foundation level. No blocking ownership gaps remain. M1.6 is not necessary. Further cognitive models should not be added without concrete evidence from repository use or Manifestation experience.

This report records the closure decision. It is not a new semantic owner.

## 2. M1 Scope

M1 established the Foundation-level cognitive semantics for:

- [Questions and Inquiry](../thinking/QUESTIONS_AND_INQUIRY_MODEL.md);
- [Hypothesis and Experiment](../thinking/HYPOTHESIS_AND_EXPERIMENT_MODEL.md);
- [Observation](../thinking/OBSERVATION_MODEL.md);
- [Evidence, uncertainty, and evidential interpretation](../thinking/EVIDENCE.md);
- [Decision and commitment](../method/DECISION_MODEL.md);
- [Design Methodology integration](../method/DESIGN_METHODOLOGY.md);
- [Foundation and Manifestation relationship semantics](../FOUNDATION_SPEC.md);
- non-pipeline cognitive topology through the [Thinking Model](../thinking/README.md).

M1 preserves loops, reversals, branching, unresolved uncertainty, inconclusive outcomes, and decisions under incomplete evidence. It does not define a mandatory universal cognitive pipeline.

## 6. M1.6 Decision

M1.6 NOT NEEDED.

No essential Foundation-level capability lacks sufficient ownership. The main cross-cutting capabilities are intentionally distributed. Residual specialization areas do not justify new semantic owners. Additional cognitive models would increase model proliferation risk.

## 7. Residual Non-Blocking Notes

Non-blocking future checks may include:

- repository-level consistency review;
- validation through real Manifestation use;
- targeted clarification only when concrete evidence shows semantic drift.

These notes do not define a roadmap or a new milestone.

## 8. Reopening Criteria

M1 should be reopened only when repository use or Manifestation experience demonstrates a concrete semantic ownership gap, conflicting canonical interpretation, or recurrent cross-domain fragmentation that cannot be resolved within existing ownership boundaries.

Reopening may be justified by:

- concrete semantic ownership gap;
- conflicting canonical interpretations;
- recurring cross-domain semantic fragmentation;
- evidence that an intentionally distributed capability has become incoherent;
- inability of existing owners to absorb a clarification without semantic distortion.

Reopening is not justified by:

- desire for symmetry;
- aesthetic completeness;
- existence of more specialized terminology;
- a local request from a single Manifestation;
- the theoretical possibility of creating another model.

## 9. Next Architectural Posture

Pause semantic expansion.

Prefer stabilization and validation through real Manifestation use.

Any future semantic milestone should be evidence-driven, not sequence-driven.
