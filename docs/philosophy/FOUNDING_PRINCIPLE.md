# Founding Principle

> La conoscenza ha valore solo quando rende le persone più autonome.

This is the founding principle of VAKI.

Knowledge is not an end in itself. Its value is realized when it increases the capacity of people to understand, decide, act, build, maintain, repair, adapt, and learn without unnecessary dependence.

Autonomy does not mean isolation. It means reducing opaque dependence, making systems understandable, preserving the ability to choose, and keeping knowledge transferable.

Every VAKI manifestation should therefore be judged not only by what it produces, but also by what it enables people to understand and do afterward.

A system that works but cannot be understood, adapted, or maintained creates dependence. A system that exposes its reasoning, constraints, evidence, and trade-offs can create autonomy.

VAKI exists to move in that direction.
