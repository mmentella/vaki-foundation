# VAKI Manifesto

We believe knowledge should create autonomy, not dependence.

We believe understanding is more durable than instruction.

We believe complex systems should be made observable, explainable, testable, and evolvable.

We believe decisions should expose their assumptions, constraints, evidence, and trade-offs.

We believe experimentation is a disciplined conversation with reality.

We believe negative results are valuable when they are preserved honestly and made reusable.

We believe prototypes are tools for learning, not excuses for permanent fragility.

We believe simplicity is achieved through understanding, not through hiding complexity.

We believe tools should serve thought, not replace it.

We believe documentation is part of the system when it preserves meaning, decisions, and operational knowledge.

We believe a product is never only its artifact. It is also the knowledge required to understand, operate, maintain, repair, adapt, and evolve it.

We believe systems should be designed for change because reality will invalidate assumptions.

We believe local implementation freedom is compatible with shared foundational principles.

We believe different manifestations may use different technologies, processes, and architectures while remaining part of the same VAKI ecosystem.

We build to learn.

We learn to decide.

We decide to build better systems.

And we preserve what we learn so that the next person starts from a higher level of autonomy.
